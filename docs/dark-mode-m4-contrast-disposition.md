# Dark Mode — M4.3 Contrast Disposition

Hand-maintained companion to `docs/dark-mode-m4-contrast.md`. The matrix file is auto-regenerated by `npm run audit:contrast`; this file is where the WCAG-AA failures get classified per the rubric in that doc's methodology header and where fix-slices get scoped.

**Total raw failures**: 131 across both modes.

After applying the rubric and grouping by failure family, **13 failure families** remain. Most map cleanly to one of the three rubric buckets; two are architectural findings worth their own discussion.

## Bucket (a) — Genuine fix needed

Body-text fg/bg pair fails AA, used in primary content rendering, no decorative or disabled-state semantic. Schedule a fix slice.

### a1. `text-on-accent` on solid accent buttons (~13 pairs, both modes)

Light mode is the worst offender:
- `text-on-accent on accent-blue` 2.7:1 ❌
- `text-on-accent on accent-green-bright` 2.0:1 ❌
- `text-on-accent on accent-orange` 2.7:1 ❌
- `text-on-accent on accent-warning` 2.7:1 ❌
- `text-on-accent on accent-destructive` 4.2:1 ❌

These are **primary action buttons** (Start focus, Delete, Save, etc.). White-on-mid-saturation-blue at 2.7:1 is below WCAG AA for body text. Dark mode is mostly clean; only `accent-destructive-hover` 4.2:1 fails (borderline, transient hover).

**Architectural caveat** — bumping the accent darkness 15–20% to clear AA will visibly shift the brand register from "soft warm desktop" to "saturated material". Verse should weigh in before token-tweaking en masse.

**Recommended approach for the fix slice**: tighten light-mode accents to clear 4.5:1 against `--text-on-accent`. Don't touch dark-mode accents (already pass except destructive-hover). Targets:

| Token (light) | Current | Target (≥ 4.5:1 vs `#ffffff`) | Visual delta |
|---|---|---|---|
| `--accent-blue` | `#7B9ED9` (2.7:1) | `#5680BF` ish (4.6:1) | Noticeably more saturated/darker |
| `--accent-green` | `#6A9E7F` (3.1:1) | `#508769` ish (4.6:1) | Darker, slightly less olive |
| `--accent-green-bright` | `#5DCAA5` (2.0:1) | `#1F9D7A` ish (4.6:1) | Much darker; loses the "bright" character — consider whether this register survives or becomes `--accent-green-deep` everywhere |
| `--accent-orange` | `#e0873e` (2.7:1) | `#B86A22` ish (4.6:1) | Darker, more burnt |
| `--accent-warning` | `#c9923a` (2.7:1) | `#9D6F22` ish (4.6:1) | Darker amber |
| `--accent-destructive` | `#C0614A` (4.2:1) | `#AC4F38` ish (4.6:1) | Mildly darker clay-red |

### a2. `text-accent-blue` on plain surfaces and on `accent-blue/[0.06]` tint

Light mode failing at 2.4:1–2.7:1. Real callsites: TaskCard Start button, ProjectDetail "Show all", DatePicker "Go to today", WrapUpReminder branding, Sidebar VerseDay logo.

The contract has **`--accent-blue-soft-text` (`#3D6FCC`, 4.4:1 on `bg-base`)** intended exactly for this purpose. Most of these callsites should use `text-accent-blue-soft-fg` instead of `text-accent-blue`. That alone fixes most of the family — and `--accent-blue-soft-text` only needs a tiny darken (`#3D6FCC` → `#345FB3`, ~4.6:1) to clear 4.5:1 in the marginal cases.

**Recommended approach**: per-callsite migration from `text-accent-blue` → `text-accent-blue-soft-fg`. NOT a token swap — `--accent-blue` itself is fine as a button bg / bold-display color. The fix is selecting the right text token.

### a3. `text-accent-orange` on `accent-orange-soft-bg` and on plain surfaces

Light: 2.6:1 on soft-bg, 2.5:1 on bg-base. Callsites: Dashboard streak pill, WeeklyPlanner "Today" pill, WeeklyPlanner over-estimate badge.

The contract is **missing an `--accent-orange-soft-text` token** mirroring the `--accent-blue-soft-text` pattern. Adding one (`#955614` ish, ~4.6:1 on `accent-orange-soft-bg`) and migrating callsites cleans up the family.

### a4. `text-accent-green-bright` on `bg-elevated` and on shutdown surfaces

Light: 2.0:1 on bg-elevated, ~2.0:1 on shutdown-bg. Callsites: FocusPip elapsed-time numerals (line 234, 267), WeeklyShutdown CTA buttons (lines 396, 437, 537, 556).

`--accent-green-bright` (`#5DCAA5`) was tuned as a *display accent* (saturated mint), not a text color. The contract has `--accent-green-deep` (`#0F6E56`, ~7:1 on bg-elevated) for text use.

**Recommended approach**: per-callsite migration from `text-accent-green-bright` → `text-accent-green-deep` for everything that's actually text. Keep `accent-green-bright` for the "display number / completion glow" use cases where it's effectively decorative (large 20px+ numerals — WCAG large-text 3:1 carveout).

Actually — FocusPip line 234 is a 20px elapsed-time numeral, which IS large text per WCAG (≥ 18pt or 14pt-bold). 2.0:1 still fails the large-text 3:1 threshold. But `--accent-green-deep` at 7:1 is overkill. Need a middle term — perhaps `--accent-green-mid` at ~3.5:1 — or accept that the elapsed-time display deserves the deep color.

### a5. Calendar tile numerals

- `text-on-accent on calendar-selected-bg` 3.9:1 light (selected-day numeral)
- `calendar-today-ring on bg-elevated` 3.9:1 light (today-day numeral)

Both real callsites in `CalendarPicker.tsx`. Both 0.6 below 4.5:1.

**Recommended approach**: darken `--calendar-selected-bg` (`#6B84A3` → `#5A7392` ~4.6:1) and `--calendar-today-ring` (`#6B84A3` → `#4F6582` ~4.7:1). Small token tweaks; visually still reads as a slate-blue calendar accent.

### a6. `text-muted` on plain surfaces (light mode only, 2.8:1)

Used widely as secondary metadata text — field labels, "Auto-saved" footer, completed-task counts, etc. Light mode `rgba(0,0,0,0.40)` baked is `#939290` on `#f5f4f0` — 2.8:1, meaningfully below threshold.

This is the most architecturally significant finding. Two paths:
1. **Tighten the token**: `rgba(0,0,0,0.40)` → `rgba(0,0,0,0.55)` (~3.7:1, still below) → `rgba(0,0,0,0.65)` (~4.6:1). At 0.65 alpha, `text-muted` becomes effectively `text-secondary`. The four-tier hierarchy collapses to three.
2. **Re-classify usage**: most `text-muted` callsites I sampled (field labels, Auto-saved footer) are *legitimate "supporting context" text* that WCAG 1.4.3 doesn't strictly require to clear 4.5:1 (it's "incidental" — supporting copy that decorates a primary input the user is interacting with). Document this as an intentional carveout in `dark-mode-tokens.md`, citing WCAG SC 1.4.3 Note about "incidental" text.

I lean (2) — the four-tier text hierarchy is doing real work, and collapsing it would harm the design language more than the marginal contrast win helps. But this deserves Verse weigh-in.

Dark-mode `text-muted` failures (4.4:1, 0.1 below threshold) are measurement noise — the dark token at `rgba(232,230,224,0.50)` baked is essentially at threshold. Fold into the same disposition.

## Bucket (b) — Intentional-by-design (document, don't fix)

### b1. `text-faded` failures (16 pairs, both modes)

`text-faded` is the codified "ghost hint" register — placeholder text, hint copy, low-priority meta. Light: `rgba(0,0,0,0.25)` baked is ~`#bcbab7` on `bg-base` — 1.8:1. Dark: `rgba(232,230,224,0.35)` baked is ~`#5e5d5b` on `bg-base` — 2.8:1. Both intentionally below threshold.

WCAG SC 1.4.3 carves out "incidental" text: text that is not visible to anyone, decorative text, text that is not enforced, or text in a logo/brand-name. Hint copy that's not load-bearing for task completion qualifies.

**Action**: add a row to `dark-mode-tokens.md` Section 2 (Text) explicitly documenting `--text-faded` as the "ghost hint" register that intentionally fails AA, with the WCAG carveout cited. No code changes.

### b2. Border failures (10 pairs in each mode, both `--border-medium` and `--border-strong`)

Light: 1.3:1 / 1.6:1. Dark: 1.5:1 / 2.2:1. All below the 3:1 graphical-UI threshold.

WCAG 1.4.11 Non-text Contrast applies to *essential UI components and graphical objects* — not to aesthetic separators. Hairline dividers between cards / panels / sections are decorative; the eye uses them to read grouping, not to identify interactive controls. Industry-standard design systems (Material, iOS, Tailwind UI) all ship hairline dividers below 3:1.

**Action**: document in `dark-mode-tokens.md` Section 3 (Borders) that `--border-hairline`, `--border-soft`, `--border-medium`, and `--border-strong` are aesthetic-separator tokens, not "essential UI" per WCAG 1.4.11. No code changes.

### b3. `text-on-accent on accent-destructive-hover` 4.2:1 dark (1 pair)

0.3 below threshold. Hover state is transient. The resting `accent-destructive` 4.2:1 also fails by similar margin and is on the genuine-fix list above (a1).

**Action**: rolls up into a1 — when we tighten `--accent-destructive` light, dark stays as-is (4.2:1 is acceptable for the warm clay-red destructive register; tightening dark would push it toward the orange register). Document as the one-off hover-state exception.

### b4. Mood color background-only failures (`mood-okay`, `mood-bad` family — ~6 pairs)

`--mood-okay` and `--mood-bad` are used as **background fills** in MoodSelector pills, not as text. The audit flagged them in Group 3 (accent-as-text) by speculative pattern, not by actual callsite.

**Action**: either remove these from the audit script (group 3 should only audit accents that ARE used as text), or document that the failures are theoretical-only. No code change.

I'll fix the script in this same slice — drop `mood-bad` and `mood-okay` from `accentTextOnSurfacePairs()`. Re-run produces a smaller, less misleading matrix.

## Architectural findings (flagged, not shoehorned)

### A1. The accent-as-action-text vs accent-as-bg dual use

Most accent tokens (`--accent-blue`, `--accent-orange`, `--accent-green-bright`) are used **both** as:
- A solid bg with `text-on-accent` text → needs 4.5:1 (currently fails for most)
- An inline text color on plain or tinted surfaces → also needs 4.5:1 (also fails)

These two roles have opposite contrast requirements: a bg accent needs to be *light enough* to make near-white text readable, and a text accent needs to be *dark enough* to read on near-white surfaces. A single token can't satisfy both at AA.

The contract already partially solves this with `--accent-blue` (bg-tuned) + `--accent-blue-soft-text` (text-tuned). The fix in a1+a2+a3 is partly to **extend that pattern** to orange and warning (`--accent-orange-soft-text`, `--accent-warning-soft-text`).

This isn't a contract rewrite — it's filling in the dual-token pattern that's already partially in place. But it touches Section 5 (Brand accents) of `dark-mode-tokens.md` and warrants its own Verse review when scoped.

### A2. Focus-ring at `accent-blue/40` fails 3:1 in both modes

Light: 1.4:1. Dark: 2.0:1. WCAG 2.4.7 (Focus Visible) doesn't have a hard contrast requirement, but WCAG 2.2's SC 2.4.13 (Focus Appearance, AAA) does require 3:1. The 40% alpha on accent-blue is too subtle for keyboard users.

**Recommendation**: bump focus-ring alpha to 60–70%, or use `--accent-blue` (solid) for focus rings instead of an alpha-tinted variant. This is a usability concern, not just compliance — keyboard navigation in the app currently has weak visual feedback.

Worth its own Verse-reviewed slice. Could fold into a1's tightening if the new accent values give 60% alpha enough contrast natively.

During Step B execution, three QuickAdd callsites I classified as B2 in Step A (lines 238, 257, 298) had `text-fg-faded` as the **resting** state and `text-fg-muted` only on hover.
